Divisions

Division (n): a major unit or section of an organization, typically one handling a particular kind of work.

What's an NT@B Division?

Strictly speaking, it's where magic happens.

Our four divisions are dynamic groups of ten to twenty supported by two leads. These are the teams that build brain-reading apps, connect with major industry powers, and write Medium's leading essays.

What's more, divisions exist as much for each other as they do for themselves. Our writers inspire our coders and our tinkerers push for outreach events. They pool their resources to teach valuable skills. Learning, building, and exploring together: this is the heart of Neurotech@Berkeley.

To see a showcase of some of our divisions' latest magic, follow the link below:

Learn More

But less strictly speaking...

And perhaps more accurately speaking, our divisions are communities. After all, they're full of people with whom we've laughed a lot and struggled a lot. Lots of us, in the course of those struggles and laughs, have become close friends.

This community aspect—that is, ensuring a diverse, inclusive one—is much more challenging in some ways than bringing a bunch of smart people together and telling them to build something. Yet it is something that every NT@B member would tell you is an integral part of why they stay.

Introducing the Divisions

The Publication Division

The world of neurotechnology changes as fast as thought. Neurotech@Berkeley's publication division covers the latest in the industry, writing on bioethics, data privacy, and technical innovations.

On Medium, Publications has been recognized as a Top Writer in Innovation and published on curated Neuroscience pages. Its in-house production, Mind, is a collaboration between NT@B, the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute, and various Berkeley faculty and graduate students.

  • Leads: Master-of-Pens: Christopher Zou. Resident Vegan: Jwalin. Design: Amy Wang.
  • Profile: A bunch of CS/MCB majors turned newspaper nerds.

Venture: Software

Neurotechnology's potential to help people is unmatched. Neurotech@Berkeley's software division is committed to building innovative, impactful software solutions to improve lives.

Each year, we participate in our parent organization NeurotechX's Open Challenge where we are tasked with using EEG data to build a software-based solution to a neuroscience problem. Our latest project, StayWoke is an Android application that alerts drivers when they are falling asleep.

At our core, we are dedicated to fostering a community of like-minded students, all in constant pursuit of furthering our understanding of neuroscience and helping patients.

  • Leads: Team Leads: Saarang Panchavati, Abhinav Pottabathula.
  • Profile: A motley crew of coders and bioligists trying to understand brains.
